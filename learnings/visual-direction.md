# Visual direction

## What is easy to get wrong

It is easy to assume the player model should eventually become a more realistic humanoid, or to treat pill-shaped characters like a temporary placeholder.

## Correct understanding

- Pill-shaped player models are part of the intended goofy-tech identity.
- They should be treated as a deliberate visual choice, not an unfinished stand-in for realistic soldiers.
- The game should lean into toy-like readability and funny silhouettes without losing clear gameplay readability.
- Pills should still have distinct character features, such as visors, hats, and other accessories, so they have personality instead of reading like anonymous capsules.

## Visual target

Future character and presentation work should generally prefer:

- bold, simple pill silhouettes
- readable forms at a glance
- shapes that feel a little silly on purpose
- distinct accessories and facial/helmet-level features that help each pill read as a character
- a mix of playful bodies and sharper, more technical weapon or movement presentation

## Avoid

- "fixing" pill characters into generic realistic humanoids by default
- sanding off the weirdness to make the project feel more serious
- proportions that make the cast look tacticool, gritty, or milsim-adjacent

## Future changes should preserve

- pill-shaped players as a core part of the game's personality
- visual clarity alongside goofiness
- distinctive pill characters rather than blank generic capsules
- the contrast between funny character shapes and crisp mechanical feel
